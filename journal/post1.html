Form best practices

Joshua Dedios September 09, 2025

For our first learning journal post, we were asked to look at the best practices for form design articles and give our thoughts on the article. The article itself goes over proper use cases and best practices for the UX of forms. It notably spends a good amount of time stressing the importance of clarity when it comes to the function of forms. The article in particular stresses ideas like:

  • Proper label positioning
  • Limiting placeholder text to exclusively being inline
  • Adding clear action signals via color
  • Adding visibility like show or hide password
  • Adding context information (like why information is being gathered)

A form that adheres to the article’s guidelines that I appreciate comes in the form of Pinterest's signup form. Pinterest’s placeholder text is inline, but isn’t exclusively inline. The parameters in which the user needs to create their password for is laid out clearly, and they have a clear action button via color.
